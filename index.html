Elevator pitch

Main ideas

  • Mobile is much less predictable than desktop.
  • There are tens of thousands of devices that can connect to the internet, each with unique capabilities.
  • We must build for all, instead of optimizing for one or two.
  • Simple and universal is the best approach.

Native apps

  • Some phones have their own platform to build apps.
  • You may have heard of: iPhones, Android, WebOS
  • Building "apps" takes longer, requires more expertise, and costs more.
  • Your app will look and feel like part of the phone.
  • Each user must download the app before using it.

Mobile websites

  • All can access the web equally!
  • Building a website is faster, free to learn, and cheaper!
  • A well-built mobile site is an "on-demand app" - it loads immediately when you visit a URL.
  • Small downside: looks weird on desktop, so you have to build a website for each.
  • Much cheaper than apps.

Responsive websites

  • A way of merging your desktop and mobile website into one package.
  • More work than a simple mobile site, but also more flexible.
  • Handles the most diverse set of devices (like tablets)
  • Still cheaper than apps.

Building your website

Make people take action

Keep it simple

  • You want people to take a specific action on your website.
  • Donating
  • Pledging
  • Volunteering
  • Re-broadcasting your message
  • Make these EASY

Don't do any of this

Don't

  • Put too much text on the homepage.
  • Use Flash. It's over.
  • Huge images / large downloads.
  • Put important info in an image.
  • Use a bunch of social widgets.

Do all of this

Do

  • Use short, concise language
  • Call people to action ("Donate now!")
  • Use big, unmistakable buttons
  • Provide telephone number, address/map, hours of operation in text, not images.
  • Link to your social hubs (Twitter, FB, etc)

the Key to Success

Performance

(send this info to your developer)

  • People are impatient. The site must load fast!
  • Mobile performance is trickier than desktop websites.
  • Loads of resources available to help with this.
  • Examples: YSlow, mobilewebbestpractices.com

Some universal webperf rules

(send to your developer)

  • Save images at the smallest acceptable size. Don't scale down using HTML or CSS.
  • Move your scripts to the bottom of the page. Keep CSS at top.
  • Don't use a ton of JS. It slows the page down and drains the user's battery.
  • If possible, keep the markup very simple and lean.
