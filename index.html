Elevator pitch

Main ideas

  • Mobile is much less predictable than desktop.
  • There are tens of thousands of devices that can connect to the internet, each with unique capabilities.
  • We must build for all, instead of optimizing for one or two.
  • Simple and universal is the best approach.

Mobile experiences

Native apps

  • Some phones have their own platform to build apps.
  • You may have heard of: iPhones, Android, WebOS
  • Your app will look and feel like part of the phone.
  • Building native apps takes longer, requires more expertise, and costs more. (Pay for each version)
  • Each user must download the app before using it.

Mobile websites

  • All can access the web equally
  • Building a website is faster, free to learn, and cheaper!
  • A well-built mobile site is an "on-demand app" - it loads immediately when you visit a URL.
  • Much cheaper than apps. Many CMSs have drop-in solutions.
  • Can look odd on desktop, so you have to build a website for each, and redirect.

Examples: m.cnn.com, m.nbcsports.com

Responsive websites

  • A way of packaging your desktop and mobile websites together. The website is built to respond to the device that displays it. Can be based on screen size, GPS, or dozens of other device features.
  • Handles the most diverse set of devices (including phones, tablets, TVs, projectors...)
  • Some kinks have not yet been worked out, like how to handle IAB and other advertising standards.

Reference: Responsive web design, Mobile-first, Rethinking the mobile web

Examples: Four Kitchens, ICANN.org

Building your website

Make people take action

Keep it simple

  • You want people to take action on your website.
  • Donating
  • Pledging
  • Volunteering
  • Re-broadcasting your message
  • Make these EASY

Don't

  • Put too much text on the homepage.
  • Use Flash. It's over.
  • Load huge images / force large downloads.
  • Put important info in an image.
  • Use a bunch of social widgets.

Do

  • Use short, concise language
  • Call people to action ("Donate now!")
  • Use big, unmistakable buttons
  • Provide telephone number, address/map, hours of operation in text, not images.
  • Link to your social hubs (Twitter, FB, etc)

Why worry about site speed?

  • Mobile users can be impatient, distracted easily.
  • Sometimes you only have a few seconds of their attention, so each one is precious.
  • The site must load fast!
  • Mobile performance is trickier than desktop websites.
  • Loads of resources available to help with this.
  • YSlow tells you why your site is slow, recommends fixes.
  • mobilewebbestpractices.com is a great starting point for creating mobile sites: strategy, design, development.
  • mobile performance links/tools from Steve Souders, mobile performance guru at Google
